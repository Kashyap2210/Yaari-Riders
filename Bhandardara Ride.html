Bhandardara Ride: Where Our Adventure Began

  • The Bhandardara Ride marked the inaugural journey for our group, laying the foundation for what would become the Yaari Riders. It was a collective first for most of us, and our lack of experience quickly became apparent. Despite being ill-prepared with inadequate rain gear and mostly stock bikes, we pushed forward undeterred, braving the soaking rain along Ghodbunder road. Navigating the highways proved challenging, with numerous rough patches slowing our progress. Departing at 4:30 am from National Park, we finally arrived at Bhandardara Dam by 1 pm, a grueling 8.5-hour journey. A fortunate stop at a roadside hotel provided us with a hearty breakfast, a stroke of luck as we later discovered a scarcity of eateries near our destination. In fact, upon arrival, we found only a solitary stall within a 3-kilometer.
  • After enjoying the dam, taking lots of photos, and feeling happy about completing our first ride successfully (even though some of us fell but quickly got back up), we all gathered at the food stall and ate almost everything. Then, we decided to start our journey back at 2:30 PM.
  • As we began our return journey, we encountered light rain near the dam, but it seemed like the roads we came from had endured heavy rainfall, leaving them filled with potholes. Despite the slight rain, the conditions made us completely drenched. It took us nearly an hour to traverse just 15 kilometers of rough terrain before connecting to the state highway. After overcoming these challenges, we stumbled upon a welcoming restaurant where we intended to grab a quick refreshment break. However, what was meant to be a brief stop turned into a leisurely lunch break lasting an hour and a half. By the time we resumed our journey, it was nearing 4 PM, with many of us riding with passengers. With the evening approaching, we aimed to cover as much distance as possible before darkness fell, planning to regroup once night descended.
