Saputara Ride: Our 1st Official Ride

  • After the Bhandardara ride, the Yaari Riders officially came into existence. The founding members were Kashyap Solanki, Siddharth Rami, Shivam Gupta, Meet Desai, and Dhairya Galiya. Subsequently, numerous other riders joined the group, expanding its membership.
  • The Saputara 3-day Independence Ride was our very first official trip as the Yaari Riders. We were thrilled to welcome three new members: Dhaval Shah, Shubham Kale. This trip is incredibly special to us because it marked the beginning of our group's adventures. During this memorable ride, we proudly displayed our group's logo, which you can see in the pictures below.
  • On the first day of our ride, we kicked off our journey from Sanjay Gandhi National Park, our designated meeting point for rides originating from the Gujarat side. Once all riders had gathered, we embarked on our three-day adventure. For this trip, we opted for a different route to Nashik, considering the poor condition of the Mumbai-Thane-Bhiwandi-Nashik route. Instead, we chose to travel via Mumbai, Manor, Trimbak, Nashik, and finally reached Saputara.
  • We arrived at Trimbak by 9:30 AM and quickly freshened up before heading for darshan. However, due to the long queue and our tight schedule—needing to visit Nashik and find accommodation in Saputara—we decided to offer our prayers from outside, watching the darshan on the TV screen and seeking blessings from Mahadev. Following the darshan, we indulged in a hearty brunch before packing up and setting off for Nashik. By 1 PM, we had reached Nashik, where we spent some time at Ramkund, relishing the refreshing waters of the Godavari River. Afterward, we resumed our journey towards Saputara.
  • As we journeyed towards Saputara, we encountered relentless rain along the way. Fortunately, we had prepared for such situations and had equipped all our luggage and riding gear with rain liners, proving to be incredibly useful. However, our preparations didn't account for the unexpected challenge of finding accommodation upon reaching Saputara on August 14, 2022. With it being Shravan month and a long weekend, there was a significant influx of visitors, particularly due to the proximity to Trimbakeshwar. After a two-hour search, we finally secured a room in a hotel, which seemed to be staff quarters, but it served our purpose nonetheless. After freshening up, we ventured out for dinner and took a leisurely stroll around the hill station, enveloped in fog, which added to the enchanting atmosphere. Returning to our hotel, we called it a day, satisfied with our adventures despite the initial challenges.
  • On the second morning, we were greeted with a rather alarming surprise. Upon awakening, we discovered water seepage on the electric board, with water trickling inside from the acrylic sheet wall. Additionally, there was dripping water from the top in the bathroom. Recognizing the potential danger, we quickly decided to change our plans and head to Vapi for the night, grateful for the swift resolution to avoid any further risks.
  • Once we reached Vapi, we found a hotel and just relaxed. We forgot about work and had fun together, enjoying our time away from the city. We laughed and talked about our experiences from the day before, simply enjoying each other's company without any stress.
  • After our stay in Vapi, we departed for Mumbai on August 16th, enjoying a hearty Gujarati breakfast of Jalebi-Fafda, Samosa, Khaman, and Kadhi. Despite encountering poor road conditions on the national highway, we persevered. Near Virar, we paused at McDonald's for a quick lunch or snack. Upon reaching National Park, we captured a final group photo marking the end of our journey. With memories of our adventure still fresh, we bid farewell and dispersed to our respective homes, grateful for the camaraderie and unforgettable experiences shared during our trip.
